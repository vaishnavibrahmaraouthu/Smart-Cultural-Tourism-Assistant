Traditional Foods

Misal Pav

A spicy curry made from sprouted moth beans, topped with farsan, onions, and served with pav (bread).

Puran Poli

Sweet flatbread stuffed with lentil and jaggery filling, especially made during festivals.

Modak

Steamed dumplings filled with coconut and jaggery, Lord Ganesha’s favorite sweet.

Traditional Dresses

Nauvari Saree

A nine-yard saree worn by Maharashtrian women, symbolizing grace and power.

Dhoti Kurta

The traditional attire for men, often paired with a turban known as the “Pheta”.

Festivals of Maharashtra

Ganesh Chaturthi

The grand celebration of Lord Ganesha’s birth with devotion, colors, and processions.

Gudi Padwa

The Maharashtrian New Year, marked by colorful decorations, food, and joy.

Diwali

The festival of lights, celebrated with diyas, sweets, and traditional clothing.

Traditional Art

Warli Painting

Famous tribal art form using white geometric shapes to depict village life and nature.

Paithani Weaving

Elegant silk sarees from Paithan, known for their rich gold borders and peacock motifs.
