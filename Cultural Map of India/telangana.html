Traditional Foods

Hyderabadi Biryani

A royal dish made with aromatic basmati rice, tender meat, and rich spices – the pride of Telangana cuisine.

Sarva Pindi

A crispy rice flour pancake with peanuts, curry leaves, and spices – a favorite breakfast snack.

Sakinalu

Crunchy rice flour rings seasoned with sesame – traditionally made during Makar Sankranti.

Traditional Dresses

Pochampally Saree

Famous handwoven sarees featuring intricate Ikat patterns, symbolizing Telangana’s weaving heritage.

Dhoti Kurta

Traditional men’s wear, often worn during cultural functions and temple festivals.

Festivals of Telangana

Bathukamma

A floral festival where women create flower arrangements and sing folk songs celebrating nature.

Bonalu

A vibrant festival dedicated to Goddess Mahankali, marked by rituals, music, and colorful processions.

Sankranti

The harvest festival celebrated with kite flying, traditional sweets, and joy across villages and towns.

Traditional Art

Cheriyal Scroll Painting

A unique storytelling art form with vivid colors and mythological scenes painted on scrolls.

Nirmal Painting

Elegant paintings known for gold backgrounds and delicate nature-inspired designs.

Monuments

charminar

Located in Hyderabad, Charminar is a 16th-century monument built by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. Its four grand arches and minarets symbolize Hyderabad’s rich history and architectural beauty.

Golconda

Golconda Fort in Hyderabad is a massive citadel known for its advanced acoustics and strong fortifications. It was once the capital of the Golconda Sultanate and famous for its diamond trade, including the Koh-i-Noor..

Ramappa Temple

Situated in Telangana, Ramappa Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site dedicated to Lord Shiva. It’s renowned for its intricate carvings, floating bricks, and Kakatiya-era architecture.
