Traditional Foods

Momos

Steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat, served with spicy chutney — the signature dish of Sikkim.

Thukpa

A comforting noodle soup with meat or vegetables, seasoned with Himalayan herbs and spices.

Gundruk

Fermented leafy greens cooked with onions and chilies — a tangy, nutritious delicacy of Sikkim.

Traditional Dresses

Bakhu

A traditional cloak fastened at the shoulder and waist with a belt, worn by both men and women.

Honju

A silk blouse worn under the Bakhu, adding elegance and color to the traditional Sikkimese outfit.

Festivals of Sikkim

Losar

The Tibetan New Year celebrated with traditional music, dance, and prayers marking joy and renewal.

Pang Lhabsol

Dedicated to Mount Kanchenjunga, this vibrant festival features masked dances and peace rituals.

Saga Dawa

A Buddhist festival celebrating the birth, enlightenment, and nirvana of Lord Buddha.

Traditional Art

Thangka Painting

Colorful scroll paintings depicting Buddhist deities and mandalas, crafted with devotion and detail.

Wood Carving

Fine carvings on monasteries and furniture that reflect Sikkim’s spiritual and artistic depth.
